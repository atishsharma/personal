2. Introduction & Market Potential

Introduction: Cordyceps Militaris is a high-value medicinal mushroom known for its immune-boosting and energy-enhancing properties. Unlike the wild variety, it can be cultivated in controlled laboratory conditions. The global and Indian market for Cordyceps is growing rapidly due to rising health awareness post-pandemic.

Market Potential: With increasing urbanization and health consciousness, the demand for nutraceuticals is skyrocketing. Cordyceps commands a premium price in the market (₹80,000 - ₹1,00,000 per kg). The product has huge export potential as well as domestic demand in the ayurvedic and supplement industry.

3. Manufacturing Process

The cultivation of Cordyceps Militaris involves a highly technical and sterile process as detailed below:

  1. Substrate Preparation: A mixture of brown rice and liquid nutrients (containing peptone, yeast extract, dextrose, etc.) is prepared and filled into glass jars (approx. 30-40g rice per jar).
  2. Sterilization: The filled jars are sealed and sterilized in a Vertical Autoclave at 121°C (15 psi pressure) for 20-30 minutes to eliminate any contaminants.
  3. Inoculation: Once cooled, the jars are moved to a Laminar Air Flow (LAF) cabinet. Liquid spawn (Mother Culture) is injected into each jar under strictly aseptic conditions.
  4. Dark Incubation: The jars are kept in a dark room at 20°C for 5-7 days until the mycelium fully colonizes the rice substrate (turning it white).
  5. Light Incubation (Fruiting): Jars are moved to the growing room with controlled LED lights (12 hours ON/OFF cycle), temperature (18-20°C), and humidity (80-90%). Fruiting bodies develop over 50-60 days.
  6. Harvesting & Drying: Mature fruit bodies are harvested and immediately dried using a Commercial Dehydrator to reduce moisture content below 5% for long shelf life.
